# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Backd team running internal sales demos to prospective merchants — ecommerce decision-makers, integrators, and developers evaluating BackdPayments as a BNPL provider. The audience is sophisticated: they know what good checkout looks like and have used Affirm, Klarna, Stripe, and Apple Pay before. Context is a live walkthrough or solo evaluation session, often side-by-side with a competitor demo. Their job-to-be-done is to judge whether BackdPayments would slot cleanly into their own storefront and feel as well-crafted as their other payment options.

## Product Purpose

A demo sneaker storefront ("SNKR hub") that wraps the BackdPayments BNPL widget in a credible end-to-end commerce flow. Success is a prospect leaving the walkthrough thinking *"BackdPayments is well-crafted and would feel native in my checkout."* This is a sales artifact, not a real consumer product — but the only way it succeeds is by feeling indistinguishable from one.

## Brand Personality

BackdPayments is **friendly, approachable, transparent.** Voice is plain-spoken — "Pay over time," not "Initiate amortized installment financing." Mint as the warm brand accent, navy for typographic weight, black reserved for primary actions. Rounded forms over sharp grids. Confidence comes from clarity, not from enterprise gravitas. The brand should feel like it belongs to a partner you'd cooperate with, not a vendor you'd negotiate with.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS dashboard cream.** Inter-everywhere, gray-on-card, rounded-square icon tiles above every heading, purple-to-blue gradients, the hero-metric template. The first thing a viewer should sense is "this was designed deliberately," not "this came from a starter."
- Cards nested inside cards. Identical icon-tile grids.
- Heavy fintech tropes (navy + gold, ledger-density tables). Wrong tone for *approachable*.

## Design Principles

1. **The widget shines, the storefront recedes.** Storefront chrome (navbar, product grid, page layout) is competent and quiet. The BackdPayments embedded module is where brand polish concentrates — it's the thing prospects came to see.
2. **Show, don't sell.** Real-feeling product names, real prices, real photography. No lorem ipsum, no "Buy now!" exhortation. Demo credibility comes from craft, not copy.
3. **Plain over polished.** Friendly/approachable means readable plain language. "Pay over time" beats "amortized installments." Trust is built by being understandable.
4. **Clarity over density.** Even on a product surface, the prospect needs to grasp the BNPL value at a glance. Generous breathing room over packed information.
5. **One CTA per surface.** Cart shows Checkout. Preview shows Add to Cart. Don't put the user in front of two competing primary actions on the same screen.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text contrast ≥4.5:1, large text and UI ≥3:1. Full keyboard navigation with visible focus rings on every interactive element. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — no parallax, no decorative bounces, no auto-advancing carousels. Form fields are labeled, not just placeholder-styled. The BackdPayments embedded widget is third-party HTML — contain it visually but don't override its semantics or focus order.
